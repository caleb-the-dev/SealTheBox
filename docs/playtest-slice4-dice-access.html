0 / 6 tested
How to test: Press T (or DEV button) → Go to Box → → pick the box name. Play a full match — DICE boxes reveal themselves in how the pool feels round-by-round.

Bounty Box tip: Use DEV → Force Bounty Box to jump straight to it. Check that Phoenix Down appears in your powers panel on match entry. Use Reset Marquee Set to test the once-per-run trigger again.

Tax Per Roll tip: The -1 HP cost doesn't fire in R1. Start a match and let a couple of rounds pass to feel the pressure ramp.

What you're testing: DICE boxes change what pool you get or what it costs you at round-end. Ask yourself: did the restriction create interesting decisions — or just feel like a handicap?

KEY QUESTIONS THIS SLICE IS TESTING

  1. Puzzle or frustration? (Single Die) — One die means one value per round. Does this create a focused sealing puzzle, or is it just arbitrary RNG misery?
  2. Do d8 vs d4 feel different? (Locked d8 / Locked d4) — Removing the big die vs removing the small die. Does each restriction create a noticeably different play feel, or do they blur together?
  3. Marquee moment? (Bounty Box) — Phoenix Down on entry is meant to feel like a gift. Does it land? Does the match feel special, or is the reward just a stat bump you forget about?
  4. Urgency or punishment? (Tax Per Roll) — Losing HP per round should create urgency to seal fast. Does the small tab set (2–6) make that feel achievable, or is the pressure just frustrating?
  5. Meaningful decisions? (Forced Full Commit) — You must account for all pips; leftovers deal damage. Does this create interesting sealing decisions, or does it feel like a gotcha?
  6. Did the restriction change your strategy? — Across all 6 boxes: did knowing the dice constraint push you to play differently?
